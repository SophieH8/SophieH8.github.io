Automated Garden

Summary of Project: The idea for my ISP is an automated garden, taking multiple environmental factors into account such as sunlight, humidity, etc. and automatically adjusting to always provide and optimal environment for the plants to grow. A Raspberry Pi will be used as the main computer in our ISP, using weather APIs and various sensors to determine the environmental conditions of the garden. The Raspberry Pi will then adjust (e.g., more artifical light in the absence of sunlight, more water pumped into the plants in absence of humidity, etc.) and allow for the plants to thrive.

Reason Of Consideration and Rejection: This project was actually my favorite idea out of all of them because seemed plausible and fun at the same time; however, it would have pushed me because I would have to learn how to use the raspberry pi, as well as, learn some other hardware to complete this project. In fact, I actually have most of the supplies at my house because of my dad, who I would’ve been able to ask if I ever needed help on this project because he is an engineer. Therefore, we wouldn’t really have to buy that much stuff. However, I ultimately decided to reject this proposal because I don’t know the people in the group. Although this sounds like a childish notion, I wouldn’t want to be stuck on a project the whole year not only with people who weren’t in my class but also with people I didn’t know in general.

If they do get it to execute well, than I think that this idea is very amazing and especially useful for people who can't afford in-person therapy or those that aren't yet ready to talk about their problems to a real person. Although I originally rejected this idea, our teams agreed to merge into one and form a new idea with both this chatbot and the unIQue proposals, which I think was a great idea. It further adds to both propasals.

ShelterSafe

Summary of Project: As our world changes due to climate change, natural disaster poses a far greater threat than anytime in the past. ShelterSafe aims to eliminate the problem of lack of awareness regarding shelters by prociding a hub that uses GPS signal to locate the user, and then uses cellular data to convey statistics from each nearby shelter concerning food supply, available space, and distance from each shelter. Administrator accounts will be given to non-profits and governments, who can then edit data. Citizens will only have read permissions.

Reason Of Consideration and Rejection: This proposal is actually a very good idea and very useful to the average citizen in everyday life. It takes a real current world problem and proposes a pretty well though out solution. However, it relies on the people having the certain app to relay the shelter information, but not everyone will have/get the app or have a device to get the app on. The problem with it is that it seems very complicated to make and actually achieving this goal seems really unrealistic, especially with the time constraint we are given. Therefore, I ultimately rejected this proposal because it seems unachievable given the time alotted.
